Security+ at 17. Full-time in IT since 18. Four years of hands-on work across help desk, field tech, systems administration, and security analysis - including two roles with direct DoD compliance responsibility before CMMC was on most people's radar.

At Cherokee Federal I deployed MHS Genesis across eight federal healthcare facilities. STIG-compliant Group Policies, endpoint hardening, 99.5% SLA in a mission-critical DoD environment. That's where I first understood that compliance isn't a checklist - it has to be built into the infrastructure from the start or it doesn't hold.

Most CMMC consultants have never managed a client's infrastructure.

At Resonant Technology Partners I was the lead sysadmin for 75+ enterprise clients - hybrid identity, Conditional Access, 50+ firewalls, 4,000+ endpoints. I ran NIST SP 800-171 audits. I built custom detection rules. I deployed EDR at scale. When CMMC requirements started flowing down from primes to our clients, I was the one dealing with it directly - not reading about it, not documenting it from the outside.

CMMC is a technical problem that most people treat as a documentation problem. The 110 controls are well-documented. What's hard is the implementation: scoping your actual environment, identifying what needs to change, and then building those changes so they survive a C3PAO assessment. Most consultants write the SSP and leave. I build the environment first, then document what's actually there.

I implement what I assess. The SSP reflects what's in place.

I'm currently a Security Analyst at Arctic Wolf, investigating 50+ daily alerts across cloud, endpoint, and network infrastructure. The SOC work keeps me current on real attack patterns. The CMMC work is where the consulting practice is building - first engagements are running now.
