Get in Touch View Resume

I got my Security+ senior year of high school. I was 17. Nobody told me to - I just wanted to understand how things worked. That curiosity has driven every job I've taken since.

I've been working in IT full-time since I was 18. Help desk, field technician, systems administration, security analyst. Four years of hands-on work across every layer of enterprise technology. And for most of that time, I was good at it without really understanding what I was building toward.

Eight months ago, that changed.

I was a lead sysadmin managing infrastructure for 75+ enterprise clients when it finally clicked. I started noticing a pattern in the people around me in security: they were chasing certifications, chasing titles, chasing six-figure salaries. But they had no foundation. No understanding of how the infrastructure underneath their alerts actually worked. No longevity.

I didn't want that. I wanted to go a layer deeper. The infrastructure is what everything is built on - it's the foundation - and I kept meeting security people who had completely skipped it. I decided I wasn't going to be one of them.

Around the same time I started paying close attention to AI. Not the hype - the trajectory. AI isn't new. It's been building for decades. What's new is the accessibility. And when I thought about where it was heading - the infrastructure it runs on, the security of that infrastructure, the engineers who build and own it - I knew exactly where I needed to position myself.

Cloud security is where AI meets infrastructure meets security. That intersection is where I'm building my career.

I'll be honest about something: I skipped a layer on the way up. Infrastructure, software engineering, the code layer. I went from help desk to field tech to sysadmin to security analyst without ever really building anything from scratch. Infrastructure security is how I'm closing that gap - learning to provision, secure, and ship real systems end to end. Not just operate and monitor them.

I'm targeting San Francisco and San Jose. Not because of the money - because of the people. I want to be in rooms where I'm being pushed every day, learning from engineers who've already solved problems I'm just starting to work on. The startup ecosystem there moves fast, new companies are being funded constantly, and the intersection of AI and cloud security is happening right in that environment. I want to build my career and my network there. Whatever comes after, that foundation will serve me for the rest of my life.

One more thing. I'm a native Japanese speaker - grew up bilingual. I'm actively working to get back to full conversational fluency, and at some point I want to work overseas. Tech is global. Infrastructure has no borders. Neither do I.
